I currently do research with the KUARQ group at the University of Kansas on the topic of quantum computing. To help familiarize myself with the field, I made my own quantum circuit simulator. More information can be found on the WHERE I AM page.

Right now, I work as the supplemental instructor for the introductory linear circuit analysis class at KU. As a supplemental instructor, I hold semiweekly sessions where I reteach and further explain concepts from class, guide and assist students through challenging homework questions, and provide study tips for upcoming exams.

From Sept. 2023 to Feb 2024, I led a metal detector project with KU's IEEE club. The goal was to make a few metal detecting circuits before the end of February. We were able to complete this project ontime and underbudget. The left image features a schematic from Overton, Moreland - Inside the Metal Detector (2016, Geotech Press), which served as reference for the development of the devices.
