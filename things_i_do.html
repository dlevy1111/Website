I currently do research with the KUARQ group at the University of Kansas on the topic of quantum computing. To help familiarize myself with the field, I made my own quantum circuit simulator. More information can be found on the WHERE I AM page.

Right now, I work as the supplemental instructor for the introductory linear circuit analysis class at KU. As a supplemental instructor, I hold semiweekly sessions where I reteach and further explain concepts from class, guide and assist students through challenging homework questions, and provide study tips for upcoming exams. Through this role, I believe I have helped to support many students who would have had to retake the class or otherwise dropped their major.

I am currently leading a metal detector project with KU's IEEE club. The goal is to make 2 metal detecting circuits before February 2024. We are attempting to create these devices without going overbudget. For now, we are working to create a simple metal detector using a schematic found online, but will soon (~December 2023) be designing our own version. The schematic shown is from Overton, Moreland - Inside the Metal Detector (2016, Geotech Press).
